Understanding the foundation of blockchain technology

Physical Cash

  • ✅ Tangible form
  • ✅ Direct peer-to-peer
  • ✅ Privacy
  • ❌ Limited by geography
  • ❌ Cannot be sent through digital networks

Traditional Digital Cash

  • ✅ Digital form
  • ✅ Global reach
  • ✅ Convenient
  • ❌ Requires intermediaries
  • ❌ Trackable
  • ❌ Regulated

Digital Peer-to-Peer Cash

  • ✅ Digital form
  • ✅ Direct peer-to-peer
  • ✅ No intermediaries
  • ✅ Global reach
  • ✅ Censorship resistant
  • ✅ Programmable money
  • ✅ 24/7 availability
  • ✅ Lower fees

Stages in the Evolution of Cash

  1. CASH 1.0 - Physical Cash
  2. CASH 2.0 - Custodial Digital Cash
  3. CASH 3.0 - Peer-to-Peer Digital Cash

Transition to CASH 3.0

  • Started when pseudonymous programmer Satoshi Nakamoto published a whitepaper in October 31, 2008
  • The whitepaper was entitled "Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System"
  • The Bitcoin blockchain went live on January 3, 2009

Main components of blockchain

  1. Digital signatures
  2. Proof-of-Work

What is a Digital Signature?

  • Proving ownership
  • Allowing public verification
  • Guaranteeing integrity

How to Create a Digital Signature?

  1. 1 Generate a private key
  2. 2 Derive a public key from the private key
  3. 3 Sign any message with the private key
  4. 4 Verify the signature with the public key

Hash Functions

  1. Signing hashes is usually done instead of signing raw messages
