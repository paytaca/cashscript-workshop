What is a Smart Contract?

  • It’s a piece of code that runs on the blockchain.
  • In Bitcoin Cash it's a piece of code that locks money and controls how it can be spent.
  • You can write rules like:
    • “Funds can only be spent after a certain date.”
    • “Two people must both approve before spending.”
    • “If condition X is met, send coins to Y.”
BTW, When we're talking about smart contracts we're talking about smart contracts in bitcoin cash. Think of it like a 'vault'. A regular vault only opens with the right key or code. A Bitcoin Cash smart contract vault can be programmed with custom rules about when and how it opens. For example: You can set it so the vault opens only if two out of three people turn their keys (multisig). Or it can open only after a certain date (timelock). Or even require a combination of conditions: two people must agree and it must be after a certain date. Just like a vault automatically enforces its rules—without needing a guard or a judge—the Bitcoin Cash blockchain automatically enforces the smart contract’s conditions. No one can cheat the vault: either the exact rules are met, or it stays locked. Why is this powerful? Because the can or cannot be spend based on the piece of code. It's incorruptable.
